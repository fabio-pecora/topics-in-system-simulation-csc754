Agent Modeling and Simulation:
Agent modeling and simulation is an area of research that helps us understand how autonomous agents, whether theyâ€™re people, organizations, or artificial entities, interact within complex systems. By simulating individual agents with their own decision-making processes, behaviors, and learning mechanisms, we can analyze how these interactions shape larger patterns and outcomes. This approach is widely used in artificial intelligence, economics, robotics, healthcare, and even social sciences to model everything from traffic systems to financial markets. With advancements in machine learning, multi-agent systems, and high-performance computing, simulations today are more detailed and dynamic than ever. Researchers use agent-based modeling to study real-world problems like epidemic outbreaks, supply chain disruptions, and smart city planning. As these techniques evolve, they continue to offer new insights into decision-making, optimization, and predictive analysis across different industries. For this survey, weâ€™ll explore key foundational research, recent innovations, and major applications in agent modeling and simulation. By reviewing and categorizing important papers, we hope to provide a clear picture of where this field stands today and where itâ€™s headed in the future.

  2. Macal, C. M., & North, M. J. (2009). Agent-Based Modeling and Simulation: Introduction and Practical Guide. In Proceedings of the 2009 Winter Simulation Conference. Retrieved from INFORMS Simulation Society.
    3. Comment:
    GOOD: This paper presents the Golden Region (GR) search, a novel Simulation Optimization (SO) method for continuous problems. By iteratively partitioning the feasible region and selecting promising subregions based on simulated point distributions and metamodel insights, GR aims to efficiently locate the global optimum. The authors establish theoretical convergence and demonstrate superior performance compared to established SO techniques. This work contributes to the field by extending partitioning-based approaches and integrating ranking and selection methods to improve search efficiency in high-dimensional spaces.

  3. Macal, C. M., & North, M. J. (2010). Introduction to Agent-Based Modeling and Simulation. Journal of Simulation. Retrieved from AAAI Publications.
    4. Comment:
    GOOD: This article provides an introduction to Agent-Based Modeling and Simulation (ABMS), emphasizing its generative and bottom-up nature. By modeling agents as autonomous entities interacting within their environment, ABMS effectively captures complex adaptive systems, making it useful for applications such as traffic flow, evacuation dynamics, and social behavior analysis. The authors discuss the advantages and challenges of ABMS, highlight key tools, and present case studies in social science and transportation. The paper concludes by outlining future challenges, emphasizing the need for improved validation and broader application of ABMS techniques.
